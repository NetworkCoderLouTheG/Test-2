What is St. Scholastica's Academy?


St. St. Scholastica’s Academy epitomizes a distinctive ethos deeply rooted in the Benedictine tradition, embodying the fundamental tenets encapsulated within the Rule of St. Benedict. At its core, this esteemed institution assumes the venerated role of a SCHOOL OF THE LORD’S SERVICE, signifying a profound commitment to shaping individuals prepared for a life deeply rooted in Christ and dedicated to serving both God and humanity. The educational experience fostered within these walls transcends the conventional realms of academia. It endeavors to impart a comprehensive framework that not only instills academic prowess but, more importantly, cultivates a moral compass and a sense of purpose steeped in the teachings of Christ. The hallmark of this Benedictine education is its unwavering dedication to nurturing individuals equipped to navigate the complexities of the contemporary global landscape.


Amidst the prevailing societal paradigm characterized by materialism, consumerism, and the pursuit of transient gratification, St. Scholastica’s Academy assumes a pivotal role. It stands as a beacon of enlightenment, endeavoring to fortify its students against the prevailing norms by instilling values rooted in spirituality, compassion, and ethical responsibility. In confronting the multifaceted challenges prevalent within our post-modern society—ranging from political corruption and social injustices to environmental degradation—the Scholastican emerges as an embodiment of resilience and hope. Armed with a profound dedication to Christ-centered principles, she becomes an advocate for love, compassion, and inclusivity, spreading the Gospel’s message through actions and words alike. The education offered within these halls transcends mere academic pursuits; it forges leaders imbued with a profound sense of purpose—individuals poised to become catalysts for positive change. It endeavors not only to prepare students for personal success but, more significantly, to equip them with the tools and values necessary to become agents of transformation in a world yearning for healing and compassion.

At St. Scholastica’s Academy, the Benedictine tradition isn’t just a set of principles; it's a way of life. This esteemed institution fosters a community where contemplation, prayer, and reflection intertwine with rigorous academics. The students are encouraged not only to excel in their studies but also to cultivate empathy, integrity, and a deep understanding of their interconnectedness with the world. Here, education extends beyond the confines of classrooms, nurturing a holistic development that embraces diversity, encourages critical thinking, and fosters a commitment to social justice. The Scholasticans, guided by this profound ethos, emerge as beacons of hope and change, poised to make meaningful contributions to a world hungry for compassion and integrity.

    Inspired by this vision, we commit ourselves to provide a HOLISTIC formation in Christian values, to nurture EXCELLENCE in academics and services and to advance relevant and transformative academic programs responsive to the needs of the local, national and global community.

    HISTORY


    St. Scholastica’s Academy was founded in 1925 in response to the urgent plea of Monsignor Prudencio David, then the parish priest of San Fernando. There were five Missionary Benedictine Sisters who came to pioneer the foundation of the school and they were Sister Superior Crescentia Veser, OSB, Sister Luitgard Schanbeck, OSB, Sister Rudolphine Bildstein, OSB, Sister Celsa Rieger, OSB and Sister Richarda Hermann, OSB.
    St. Scholastica’s Academy was also known as Assumption Academy of Pampanga when it was founded. It was located at the Singian residence at the foot of Baluyut Bridge in San Fernando Pampanga. In June 1925 the school opened for elementary students and the following year, the school opened for first year high school exclusive for girls. Each year, a new grade level was added that on March 1930 Assumption Academy had its first high school graduates.
    In 1931, the school transferred to its second site in poblacion of San Fernando Pampanga because of the increasing number of students in elementary and in high school. The second location of St. Scholastica’s Academy was a swampy area. In 1938 the ownership of the school was transferred from the Diocese of San Fernando to the Missionary Benedictine Sisters of Tutzing. After 40 years, in 1966, the Assumption Academy changed its name to St. Scholastica’s Academy to avoid confusion with the newly opened Assumption College now Assumption University of the Archdiocese of San Fernando Pampanga. St. Scholastica’s Academy became easily identified as a subsidiary school of St. Scholastica’s College Manila

    In 1972, the High School department of St. Scholastica’s Academy transferred to the barren grounds of Cer-Hill, Quebiawan, due to the floods in poblacion and to accommodate the fast increasing enrolment. The previously “match-like” building has been expanded into an “E – shape” building that houses the whole High School Department. Additional constructions were made: covered court, eating areas with sheds and another building to house the Grade School Department and covered walks. In 1987, a newly constructed building was blessed for the Grade School department. The pre-school later was transferred to a new building in June 2001.
    In 1996, SSA – SF suffered a tremendous drop in enrolment due to the devastation of lahar but was able to recover the following year. In 2001, the Preschool was transferred to Sto. Nino Building of the Grade School Department In 2016, the High School and Grade School departments were granted PAASCU Level III accredited status.
    With more than 2,500 enrollees, St. Scholastica’s Academy, City of San Fernando seeks its continuing role of providing its present clientele with quality Filipino Christian Benedictine education. Confronted with the present realities and challenges of our times, St. Scholastica’s Academy stands with courage to make a difference in society. Grateful for its ninety five (95) years of existence and apostolic service (1925 – 2020), SSA shares the promise of a new heaven and new earth as she “moves on and reaches out” to and with all the people it serves and works with: “That in all things God may be glorified.”
